Michael Jordan won 6 NBA championship rings with the Chicago bulls. He won his first ring in 1991, after being in the league for 7 years. Jordan won his first 3 rings from 1991 – 1993, before retiring in the summer of 1993. He then went on the play baseball for a view month, before returning to the National Basketball Association at the end of the 1995 season; however his skill level wasn’t as sharp as when he left, subsequently he and the bulls dropped out of the playoffs early. Over the summer of 1995, Michael Jordan made sure to put in all the practice, so that he can be ready for the 95/96 NBA season. Jordan and his Chicago team came out of the gates like a raging bull, and they maintained this prowess throughout the season, in the end they sealed another title, earning Jordan his 4th ring. Jordan and the Bulls then went on to win 2 more rings – 97 and 98, making it a total of 6 NBA titles in the 90’s before Jordan retired again. Jordan would return to the league a few years later playing for the Washington Wizards, a team he part owned at the time – all because of his love for the game. He did not have the same impact on the court as he did when he was younger, but he is still revered as the greatest of all time.
